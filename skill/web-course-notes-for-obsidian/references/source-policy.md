# Source Policy

Use the user's URLs as pointers to source material, not permission to bypass access controls.

## Allowed

- Read public pages that are normally accessible in the browser.
- Use user-provided transcripts, excerpts, screenshots, or downloaded files.
- Summarize, outline, compare, and create study notes from accessible material.
- Quote only short excerpts when needed for explanation or citation.
- Preserve source URLs and page titles in `source_manifest.md`.

## Not Allowed

- Bypass login, paywall, DRM, rate limits, robots restrictions, anti-bot measures, or disabled downloads.
- Reconstruct a copyrighted book, chapter, lecture transcript, or article in full.
- Mass-download videos, books, or slide decks unless the user explicitly asks and the source allows it.
- Hide source provenance or imply the notes are original when they are derived from a website.

## Authenticated Sources

If the source requires login, ask the user how they want to provide access. Prefer browser-visible pages, user-provided exports, transcripts, or files. Do not type credentials unless the user explicitly authorizes the specific site and action.

## Copyrighted Books

For book websites, produce:

- chapter outline,
- concept explanations,
- formulas and definitions,
- reading guide,
- source-linked summary,
- personal study notes.

Do not reproduce chapter text or long passages.
