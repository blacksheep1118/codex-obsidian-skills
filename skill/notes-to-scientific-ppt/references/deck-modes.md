# Scientific Deck Modes

Choose one primary deck mode before writing the slide plan. If the user does not specify a mode, infer it from the notes and record the assumption in the deck brief.

## Paper Reading

Use for literature notes, paper notes, and reading group talks.

Main spine:

- research question and why it matters,
- prior gap and assumptions,
- method intuition,
- mechanism diagram,
- equations and variable legend,
- implementation or training details,
- experiments and metrics,
- ablations and comparisons,
- limitations and discussion questions.

Required proof objects: mechanism diagram, formula bridge, experiment table or figure, comparison matrix, limitation slide.

## Proposal

Use for project proposals, research plans, and grant-like planning decks.

Main spine:

- problem and opportunity,
- evidence that the gap matters,
- hypothesis,
- proposed method,
- data and evaluation plan,
- milestones,
- risks and mitigations,
- expected contribution.

Required proof objects: problem evidence, method schematic, evaluation matrix, milestone timeline, risk table.

## Progress Report

Use for lab updates, weekly/monthly research progress, and internal project reviews.

Main spine:

- goal and current status,
- what changed since the last update,
- completed experiments,
- current evidence,
- blockers and failures,
- next decisions,
- near-term plan.

Required proof objects: status table, experiment log, result comparison, blocker/risk table, next-step decision slide.

## Teaching

Use for lectures, tutorials, and technical onboarding.

Main spine:

- concept motivation,
- prerequisite bridge,
- intuition,
- worked example,
- formula walkthrough,
- common mistakes,
- practice or discussion,
- summary.

Required proof objects: concept ladder, worked example, equation annotation, misconception table, recap slide.

## Defense

Use for thesis defenses, proposal defenses, and formal research presentations.

Main spine:

- thesis question,
- contributions,
- related-work gap,
- method,
- theoretical or design justification,
- experimental evidence,
- limitations,
- future work,
- committee questions or backup appendix.

Required proof objects: contribution map, method pipeline, equation/algorithm slide, result evidence, limitation/future-work slide, appendix index.
